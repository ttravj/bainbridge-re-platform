## How to Use This Matrix

This reference covers the 10 most populous states where agents most frequently practice. For each state, we document: permitted agency types, mandatory disclosure forms, buyer agency agreement requirements (post-settlement), seller disclosure obligations, advertising rules, CE requirements, record retention periods, and notable state-specific rules.

**Critical reminder:** State laws change. This matrix reflects requirements as of early 2026. Always verify current rules with the applicable state real estate commission before relying on this information for a specific transaction.

## California

**Regulatory Body:** California Department of Real Estate (DRE)

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent
- Dual agency legal with written informed consent from both parties (Civil Code 2079.16)
- Agency disclosure required as soon as practicable, before entering into a listing or buyer agreement

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Transfer Disclosure Statement (TDS): seller discloses all known material defects
- Seller Property Questionnaire (SPQ): supplemental questionnaire covering specific conditions
- Natural Hazard Disclosure (NHD): flood, fire, earthquake, and environmental zones
- Agency Disclosure (AD): disclose whom the agent represents
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)
- Death on property: must disclose if death occurred within 3 years
- Megan's Law disclosure: provide informational notice (not location-specific)

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written agreement required before touring any property
- Must specify compensation in objectively ascertainable terms
- Must include negotiability disclosure
- C.A.R. updated its Buyer Representation and Broker Compensation Agreement (BRBC) forms post-settlement

### Advertising Rules
- Brokerage name must appear in all advertising
- DRE license number required on all solicitation materials
- Team names must include brokerage name with equal or greater prominence
- Social media: brokerage identification required on profiles and posts that advertise services

### CE Requirements
- 45 hours every 4 years (first renewal: 45 hours including specific mandatory topics)
- Mandatory topics: ethics, agency, trust fund handling, risk management, fair housing, implicit bias (2 hours), management and supervision
- Implicit bias training added as mandatory topic in 2023

### Record Retention
- 3 years from close of transaction or listing expiration

### Notable Rules
- Attorney-in-fact provisions: agents cannot act as attorney-in-fact for clients in RE transactions without specific authorization
- Withholding of 3.33% of sale price for non-resident sellers (CA tax)
- Extremely detailed disclosure requirements -- more forms than any other state

## Texas

**Regulatory Body:** Texas Real Estate Commission (TREC)

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, intermediary (Texas version of dual agency)
- Intermediary status: broker appoints agents to each party; intermediary does not advocate for either. Requires written consent.
- No "transaction broker" designation

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Seller's Disclosure Notice: comprehensive property condition form (TREC promulgated)
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)
- Information About Brokerage Services (IABS): must be provided at first substantive dialogue

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written buyer representation agreement required before touring
- TREC updated forms post-settlement
- Must specify compensation terms, duration, and include negotiability language
- TREC-promulgated forms are mandatory -- agents cannot use non-TREC contract forms except in limited circumstances

### Advertising Rules
- Licensed business name must appear in all advertising
- TREC rules require that advertising not be misleading, deceptive, or identify the agent in a manner that could mislead
- Team advertising must include the brokerage name

### CE Requirements
- 18 hours every 2 years
- Mandatory: Legal Update I (4 hrs), Legal Update II (4 hrs), plus 10 hours elective
- Broker responsibility course required for supervising brokers

### Record Retention
- 4 years

### Notable Rules
- TREC promulgates standard contract forms -- agents must use them (no custom contracts)
- Property tax prorations are handled differently (Texas has no state income tax but high property taxes)
- Homestead protections are among the strongest in the U.S. -- affects liens, foreclosure, and disclosure

## Florida

**Regulatory Body:** Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR), Division of Real Estate

### Agency Types
- Single agent (buyer's agent or seller's agent with full fiduciary duties)
- Transaction broker (limited representation -- no fiduciary duty of loyalty or confidentiality). THIS IS THE DEFAULT in Florida.
- No brokerage relationship (customer with no representation)
- **No dual agency.** Florida eliminated dual agency and replaced it with transaction broker status.

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Disclosure of representation type: must be provided before or at the time of entering into a listing, buyer brokerage agreement, or before showing property
- Seller disclosure: Johnson v. Davis (1985 case law) requires disclosure of known material defects. Statute (475.278) codifies disclosure obligations.
- No standardized seller disclosure form at state level (many MLSs and associations provide forms)
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written agreement required before touring
- Must specify compensation and duration
- Florida forms updated post-settlement by Florida REALTORS and Florida Bar

### Advertising Rules
- Brokerage name as registered with DBPR must appear in all advertising
- Licensed name or registered trade name only
- All internet advertising must include brokerage name
- Team names must not be used in a way that misleads the public

### CE Requirements
- 14 hours every 2 years
- Mandatory: 3 hours core law, 3 hours ethics, 8 hours specialty
- Post-license: 45 hours within first renewal period for sales associates

### Record Retention
- 5 years

### Notable Rules
- Transaction broker is the DEFAULT relationship unless single agent is established in writing
- No dual agency -- if a brokerage has a listing and a buyer walks in, the default is transaction broker for both parties
- Escrow disputes: Florida has specific interpleader and escrow dispute resolution processes
- Condo/HOA: extensive disclosure requirements for condominium and HOA purchases (Condo Rider, HOA Rider)

## New York

**Regulatory Body:** New York Department of State (DOS), Division of Licensing Services

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent, designated agent (within same brokerage)
- Dual agency legal with informed written consent of both parties
- Agency disclosure form required at first substantive contact

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Property Condition Disclosure Statement (PCDS): required by statute (Real Property Law 462-A). However, seller may opt out by providing a $500 credit to the buyer at closing instead of completing the form.
- Agency Disclosure Form: must be presented and signed before any substantive discussion
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written buyer agency agreement required before touring
- Must include specific compensation terms, duration, and negotiability disclosure
- New York Association of REALTORS (NYSAR) updated forms post-settlement

### Advertising Rules
- All advertising must include the name of the brokerage firm as licensed
- DOS strictly enforces advertising rules -- regular audits
- Social media must include brokerage identification
- Team names: brokerage name must appear with equal or greater prominence

### CE Requirements
- 22.5 hours every 2 years
- Mandatory topics: fair housing (2 hours minimum), agency, ethics, legal matters
- Cultural competency component in some CE offerings

### Record Retention
- 3 years (NYSAR recommends longer)

### Notable Rules
- $500 credit in lieu of disclosure: sellers can choose to pay $500 rather than complete the PCDS -- most sellers choose the credit, creating less transparency for buyers
- Attorney state: attorneys typically involved in all closings and contract review
- Attorney review period: standard 3 business days in most contracts
- Co-op buildings: unique rules for co-op sales (board approval, flip tax, maintenance)

## Pennsylvania

**Regulatory Body:** Pennsylvania Real Estate Commission (PREC)

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent, designated agent
- Dual agency legal with written consent
- Consumer Notice: must be provided at first contact (before any real estate services)

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Seller's Property Disclosure Statement: comprehensive form covering structural, mechanical, environmental, and neighborhood conditions
- Consumer Notice: explains agency types and broker's duties
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written agreement required before touring
- Must include compensation terms, duration, and negotiability language
- Pennsylvania Association of REALTORS (PAR) updated forms post-settlement

### Advertising Rules
- Licensed brokerage name must appear in all advertising
- Team names cannot overshadow brokerage identification
- Online advertising (websites, social media) must prominently display brokerage name

### CE Requirements
- 14 hours every 2 years
- Mandatory topics determined by PREC; typically include ethics and legal updates
- Broker: 14 hours including supervision and management requirements

### Record Retention
- 3 years after completion or termination of transaction

### Notable Rules
- Consumer Notice at first contact is strictly enforced
- Transfer tax: 2% total (1% each buyer and seller), higher in Philadelphia (4.278% total)
- Attorney involvement common but not required in all areas
- Zoning/use changes: municipal-level rules create significant compliance variation

## Illinois

**Regulatory Body:** Illinois Department of Financial and Professional Regulation (IDFPR)

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent, designated agent
- Dual agency legal with written informed consent
- Designated agency allows different agents within the same brokerage to represent opposing parties

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Residential Real Property Disclosure Report: seller discloses known material defects
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)
- Radon disclosure required

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written agreement required before touring
- Must specify compensation, duration, and include negotiability disclosure
- Illinois REALTORS updated forms post-settlement

### Advertising Rules
- Brokerage name must appear in all advertising
- Team names must include brokerage name
- IDFPR can audit advertising compliance

### CE Requirements
- 12 hours every 2 years
- Mandatory: core curriculum determined by IDFPR

### Record Retention
- 5 years

### Notable Rules
- Attorney review period: 5 business days is standard practice in most Illinois contracts. Either party's attorney can modify or void the contract during this period.
- Transfer tax: state ($0.50/$500) plus county ($0.25/$500) plus municipal (varies -- Chicago is $7.50/$500 for sellers)
- Chicago has its own additional real estate ordinances (RLTO, pre-closing inspection requirements for some properties)
- Radon disclosure is mandatory (not optional)

## Ohio

**Regulatory Body:** Ohio Division of Real Estate and Professional Licensing

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent
- Dual agency legal with written informed consent
- Agency disclosure required at first substantive contact before any confidential information is shared

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Residential Property Disclosure Form: seller must complete for residential sales (1-4 units)
- Agency Disclosure Statement: presented at first substantive contact
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written buyer agency agreement required before touring
- Must include specific compensation, duration, and negotiability language
- Ohio REALTORS updated forms post-settlement

### Advertising Rules
- Brokerage name must appear in all advertising materials
- Social media profiles used for real estate must include brokerage identification
- Team advertising must not obscure the brokerage name

### CE Requirements
- 30 hours every 3 years
- Mandatory topics: civil rights/fair housing (3 hrs), core law (3 hrs), ethics (3 hrs), plus electives
- Significant mandatory component compared to many states

### Record Retention
- 3 years from date of transaction or listing termination

### Notable Rules
- Consumer Guide to Agency Relationships must be provided at first substantive contact
- Transfer tax (conveyance fee): varies by county, typically $1-$4 per $1,000
- Ohio has specific rules about handling earnest money through brokerage trust accounts
- Mineral rights: in some parts of Ohio, mineral rights are a significant disclosure issue (shale gas)

## Georgia

**Regulatory Body:** Georgia Real Estate Commission (GREC)

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent, designated agent
- Dual agency legal with written informed consent
- Designated agency explicitly authorized by statute

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Seller's Property Disclosure: NOT required by statute for most residential sales. However, most MLS systems and REALTOR associations require it as standard practice.
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)
- Brokerage Relationships in Real Estate Transactions (BRRETA): agency disclosure brochure required to be provided

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written agreement required before touring
- Must specify compensation, duration, and negotiability language
- Georgia Association of REALTORS (GAR) updated forms post-settlement

### Advertising Rules
- Brokerage name must appear in all advertising
- License number not required in advertising (unlike some states)
- Team names: must not mislead; brokerage name required

### CE Requirements
- 36 hours every 4 years
- Mandatory: 25 hours post-license for new licensees in first renewal cycle
- Continuing: includes mandatory topics set by GREC (typically ethics, license law, agency)

### Record Retention
- 5 years (GREC rule)

### Notable Rules
- Georgia follows caveat emptor tradition more than most states -- seller disclosure is not required by statute, though it has become standard practice
- BRRETA brochure on brokerage relationships is unique to Georgia
- GAR forms are the standard (not commission-promulgated like Texas)
- Transfer tax: $1 per $1,000 of sale price (seller pays)
- Intangible tax on new mortgages: $1.50 per $500 (buyer pays)

## North Carolina

**Regulatory Body:** North Carolina Real Estate Commission (NCREC)

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent, designated agent
- Dual agency legal with written consent
- Working With Real Estate Agents brochure must be provided at first substantive contact

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Residential Property and Owners' Association Disclosure Statement: comprehensive form required for 1-4 unit residential properties
- Mineral and Oil and Gas Rights Mandatory Disclosure: separate disclosure specific to NC
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)
- Working With Real Estate Agents disclosure brochure

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written agreement required before touring
- Must include compensation terms, duration, and negotiability language
- NC REALTORS and NCREC updated forms post-settlement

### Advertising Rules
- Firm name must appear in all advertising (not just agent name)
- Social media: firm name required
- Team advertising: firm name must appear prominently

### CE Requirements
- 8 hours per year (one of the few states with annual CE)
- Mandatory topics: NCREC General Update (4 hrs) + NCREC Elective (4 hrs)
- NCREC sets specific course content each year -- highly structured

### Record Retention
- 3 years from end of transaction

### Notable Rules
- **Due diligence period:** NC uses a negotiated due diligence period instead of individual contingencies. Buyer pays a non-refundable due diligence fee and has until the due diligence deadline to terminate for any reason. This is unique among major states and fundamentally changes negotiation dynamics.
- Due diligence fee: non-refundable to the seller even if buyer terminates (unlike earnest money, which is refundable during contingency periods in most states)
- Earnest money: separate from due diligence fee. Held until closing or contract termination.
- NCREC is unusually active in enforcement -- more disciplinary actions per capita than most states

## Arizona

**Regulatory Body:** Arizona Department of Real Estate (ADRE)

### Agency Types
- Buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent (limited representation)
- Dual agency legal with written consent from both parties
- Arizona uses specific consent forms for dual agency situations

### Mandatory Disclosure Forms
- Seller's Property Disclosure Statement (SPDS): comprehensive form covering all property conditions, improvements, and known defects
- Lead-Based Paint Disclosure: pre-1978 homes (federal)
- Affidavit of Disclosure: required for unsubdivided land sales (5 or fewer parcels)
- Insurance Claims History Disclosure: required for residential sales

### Buyer Agency Agreement (Post-Settlement)
- Written agreement required before touring
- Must include compensation, duration, and negotiability language
- Arizona Association of REALTORS (AAR) updated forms post-settlement

### Advertising Rules
- Licensed entity name must appear in all advertising
- ADRE monitors advertising compliance
- Team names must include brokerage name
- Virtual office (VOW) and internet advertising must include brokerage identification

### CE Requirements
- 24 hours every 2 years
- Mandatory topics: commissioner's standards, contract law, agency, fair housing, legal issues, disclosure
- Broker Management Clinic required for designated brokers

### Record Retention
- 6 years (longest of the top 10 states)

### Notable Rules
- **BINSR (Buyer's Inspection Notice and Seller's Response):** Arizona uses a specific, standardized form for inspection negotiation. Buyer submits the BINSR with repair requests, seller responds with acceptance, counter, or refusal. This structured process differs from the informal repair request approach in many states.
- SPD Statement is one of the most comprehensive seller disclosures in the country
- Arizona is an escrow state -- title companies handle closings
- No state income tax withholding for out-of-state sellers (unlike California)
- Affidavit of Disclosure for unsubdivided land is unique to Arizona
- Insurance claims history disclosure is mandatory (not common in other states)
- Community Facilities District (CFD) disclosure required where applicable -- significant in new construction areas around Phoenix
